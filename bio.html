Bio

I received a first-class BSc and a PhD, both in Mathematics, from the University of Manchester. I then taught and researched in the area of applied mathematics at Liverpool Polytechnic and the Polytechnic of North London before joining South Bank Polytechnic in 1972.

My move into economics began at South Bank as the result of collaborative research with Sam Aaronovitch into merger activity and a year studying for an MSc in economics (distinction) at Queen Mary College followed in 1977. In 1984 I became a senior research officer at the Centre for Economic Forecasting, London Business School and was appointed Professor of Economics at the University of Leicester in 1989. In 1994 I moved to the University of Surrey. I am a member of the macroeconomics research group, CIMS, and am also a visiting Professor in the Department of Economics, City University, London.
